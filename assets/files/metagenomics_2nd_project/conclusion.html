Conclusion and Acknowledgement

Conclusion

A novel network inference method using a goodness of fit to proportionality measure was compared with 8 other pre-existing inference methods for validation purposes. The proportionality method outperformed those methods in precision and reached top level of sensitivity and specificity for low sparsity datasets. The proportionality method has a limitation which is poor performance on zero-rich (or high sparsity) datasets. Future work can improve the scope of sparsity that the method can accept by handling zeros rather than discarding them. The method was succesfully used as a hypothesis generator for a stroke case control microbiome dataset. It detected genera known to be associated with stroke, and also highlighted interactions between those genera such as Pseudomonas and other genera that are not known to be associated with stroke such as Aquamonas.
